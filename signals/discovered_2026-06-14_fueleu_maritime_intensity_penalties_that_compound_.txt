FuelEU Maritime took effect 1 January 2025, requiring ships above 5,000 GT calling at EU ports to cut the greenhouse-gas intensity of their energy below a baseline that ratchets down (-2% now, steepening toward -80% by 2050), with a penalty per tonne of VLSFO-equivalent deficit that DOUBLES for each consecutive year a vessel stays non-compliant. Operators are hurt because compliance is not a filing but a fleet-wide optimisation problem — pooling surpluses across ships, banking and borrowing compliance balances, and timing biofuel/RFNBO bunkering — that the European Commission specifies but will never operate on a shipowner's behalf. The cross-jurisdiction twist is that only the EU-port leg counts, so routing, pooling and flag decisions can legally arbitrage the boundary; value grows precisely as the intensity limit and the doubling penalty bite harder.
