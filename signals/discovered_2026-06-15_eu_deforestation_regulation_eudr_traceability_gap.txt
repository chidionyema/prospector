The EUDR mandates rigorous geolocated supply chain verification for products like coffee, cocoa, and rubber, forcing exporters to prove their land-use history. Smallholder farmers, who produce much of this yield, are currently failing to generate the high-resolution, compliant digital footprints required to keep their produce in the European market. This creates a high-stakes, compounding pressure to digitize legacy farming assets or face market exclusion, an infrastructure gap that neither the EU regulator nor small exporters can bridge alone.
