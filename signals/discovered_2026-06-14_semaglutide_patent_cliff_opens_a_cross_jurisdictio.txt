Core semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) patents expire across staggered timelines — India, Brazil, Canada and China from 2026 — while remaining in force in the US and EU, so legal generic and compounded manufacture is opening in some regimes years before others. This creates a jurisdictional gap: legitimate sourcing, dosing, cold-chain and eligibility navigation across regimes that no single regulator or Novo Nordisk can or will close. The pain is acute for self-pay patients, clinics and employers facing rationing and $1,000+/month US pricing, and it grows as enforcement and import scrutiny intensify around the divergence.
