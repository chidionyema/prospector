The EU Deforestation Regulation (2023/1542 / EUDR) requires importers of cattle, cocoa, coffee, palm oil, rubber, soy and wood to prove, with plot-level geolocation and a full due-diligence statement, that goods placed on the EU market are deforestation-free — with large operators in scope from 30 December 2025 and penalties up to 4% of EU turnover. The country benchmarking system grades source nations low/standard/high risk, so the same commodity from two origins faces wildly different evidentiary burdens, creating a cross-jurisdiction gap no single authority can close. Traders, cooperatives and downstream manufacturers with tangled non-EU supply chains are newly exposed and cannot meet the geolocation-and-traceability bar with existing paperwork. Value compounds as enforcement and audits ramp, and the burden of proof sits on the regulated party, not the regulator.
