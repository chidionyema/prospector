After the 2023 and Jan-2024 renewals, global property-catastrophe reinsurance rates jumped 30-50%+ and reinsurers pushed attachment points sharply higher, structurally retreating from low-layer cat risk. Primary insurers, REITs, municipalities and large property owners now carry far bigger uninsured first-loss layers than they planned for, with no cheap way back in. The pain compounds every renewal as climate losses harden the market further, and the reinsurers causing the squeeze will never build tools that help cedents arbitrage, structure parametric/captive alternatives, or optimise their retained-layer exposure against it.
