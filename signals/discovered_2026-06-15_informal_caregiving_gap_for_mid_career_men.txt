An increasing number of men aged 55-65 are now managing a parent with dementia for the first time while simultaneously navigating the demands of full-time professional employment. This specific demographic faces a acute lack of structured, flexible support frameworks that bridge the gap between medical care and daily logistical management, leading to burnout and premature exits from the workforce. The growing intersection of workplace productivity demands and intensive caregiving responsibilities creates a significant, underserved market for specialized management and coordination services.
