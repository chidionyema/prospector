In April 2024 the US EPA set the first enforceable national drinking-water limits for six PFAS 'forever chemicals' (PFOA/PFOS at 4 parts per trillion), with monitoring required by 2027 and treatment compliance by 2029. Thousands of small and rural water systems must now test at trace levels, report results, and install costly removal technology they have no in-house capital, lab access, or engineering staff for. The newly underserved are the operators of small community water systems, who face hard federal deadlines and liability but lack the procurement, sampling-logistics, and treatment-financing capacity that large utilities take for granted.
