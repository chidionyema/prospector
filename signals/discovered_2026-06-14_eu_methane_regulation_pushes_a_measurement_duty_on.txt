The EU Methane Regulation (2024/1787) took effect in 2024 and phases in hard duties: measurement, reporting and verification obligations live from 2025, and from 2027 imported oil, gas and coal must demonstrate monitoring equivalent to EU rules, with methane-intensity limits and escalating financial penalties to follow. Non-EU producers — who supply the bulk of EU energy — must now prove granular, well-level methane performance they have never measured, a cross-jurisdiction obligation that no single national regulator can resolve. Importers, traders and their upstream suppliers face compounding penalty and contract risk as enforcement tightens year on year, so the value of defending and optimising their position grows with every enforcement step. Why now: the 2025 MRV obligations are already binding and the 2027 import standard is forcing supply-chain remediation contracts to be signed today.
