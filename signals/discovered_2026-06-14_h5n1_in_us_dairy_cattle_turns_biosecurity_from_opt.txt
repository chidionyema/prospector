Since March 2024 highly pathogenic avian influenza (H5N1) has spread to hundreds of US dairy herds across multiple states — a species jump never seen before — forcing bulk-milk testing mandates, interstate movement controls and culling that disrupt milk supply and farm cash flow. Dairy operators, milk haulers, processors and farm-labour insurers are newly exposed to a moving, enforcement-compounding biosecurity duty that USDA imposes but cannot operationally run on-farm. The opening is to defend, monitor and de-risk the herd-owner against escalating testing/quarantine regimes, and the value grows the harder the outbreak and surveillance are pushed.
